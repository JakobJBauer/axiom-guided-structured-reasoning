[HIGH-READABILITY]  
Picture a story that glides along so smoothly the words almost read themselves. A story is HIGH-READABILITY if The story has readability score above 96.1

[LOW-READABILITY]  
Now imagine the opposite: a tale that feels heavier to parse, where the sentences slow the reader down. A story is LOW-READABILITY if The story has readability score below 86.4

[STARTS-WITH-NOUN]  
Some stories kick open the door with a solid thing—an object, a person, a place—named right away. A story is STARTS-WITH-NOUN if The story starts with a noun

[STARTS-WITH-ADVERB]  
Other stories begin in motion, with a mood or manner—swiftly, quietly, suddenly—setting the scene by how events unfold. A story is STARTS-WITH-ADVERB if The story starts with a adverb

[USES-PERFECT-ASPECT]  
In certain narratives, the timeline has layers: what has happened, what had been done, what has already shifted before the present moment arrives. A story is USES-PERFECT-ASPECT if The story uses perfect aspect

[USES-DISCOURSE-MARKERS]  
And sometimes the storyteller leaves a trail of signposts—however, therefore, meanwhile—guiding the reader through turns in thought. A story is USES-DISCOURSE-MARKERS if The story uses discourse markers

[HAS-A-CLIFFHANGER]  
Then there’s the classic hook: the scene cuts at the worst (best) possible moment, leaving the reader teetering at the edge. A story is HAS-A-CLIFFHANGER if The story has a cliffhanger

[HAS-JUXTAPOSITION]  
Some tales love contrast—placing two things side by side so their differences spark meaning. A story is HAS-JUXTAPOSITION if The story has juxtaposition

[HAS-A-RED-HERRING]  
Others are tricksters, tossing in a tempting distraction that pulls the reader’s attention away from what really matters. A story is HAS-A-RED-HERRING if The story has a Red Herring

[SHORT-STORY]  
Occasionally, the entire adventure is a sprint: brief, sharp, and over before you expect it. A story is SHORT-STORY if The story has fewer than 160 words

[STYLE-PHILOSOPHICAL]  
Some narrators don’t just tell what happened—they wonder what it means, circling questions about life, choice, and truth. A story is STYLE-PHILOSOPHICAL if The story is philosophical

[STYLE-PLAYFUL]  
Others dance: light on their feet, mischievous, enjoying language and surprise. A story is STYLE-PLAYFUL if The story is playful

[STYLE-HUMOROUS]  
And some aim straight for laughter, shaping moments to amuse. A story is STYLE-HUMOROUS if The story is humorous

[THEME-AGENCY]  
In certain stories, the beating heart is Agency—the power to choose, to act, to steer fate rather than be dragged by it. A story is THEME-AGENCY if The story has the theme Agency
