Annotation rule: Mark [USES-DISCOURSE-MARKERS] = true if such markers are present and function to structure the discourse.

[HAS-JUXTAPOSITION]
Definition: A story satisfies [HAS-JUXTAPOSITION] when it contains juxtaposition.
Specification: Juxtaposition denotes the deliberate placement of contrasting or complementary elements (characters, scenes, themes, images, or ideas) in close proximity to highlight differences or relationships.
Annotation rule: Mark [HAS-JUXTAPOSITION] = true if juxtaposed elements are employed as a structural or rhetorical device.

[TOPIC-MYSTICAL-CREATURES]
Definition: A story satisfies [TOPIC-MYSTICAL-CREATURES] when its subject matter concerns mystical creatures.
Specification: “Mystical creatures” refers to beings of supernatural, mythic, or magical provenance (e.g., dragons, fairies, spirits) that are central to the narrative’s topic or premise.
Annotation rule: Mark [TOPIC-MYSTICAL-CREATURES] = true if mystical creatures are a primary topical focus.

[HAS-A-RED-HERRING]
Definition: A story satisfies [HAS-A-RED-HERRING] when it contains a red herring.
Specification: A red herring is an intentional misleading cue or false lead introduced to divert attention from the actual explanation, outcome, or perpetrator.
Annotation rule: Mark [HAS-A-RED-HERRING] = true if the narrative includes an element designed to mislead or distract from the true solution or meaning.
