Overview
- Purpose: Provide clear, structured definitions for each annotation node and the exact conditions that make a story satisfy composite nodes.
- Use: Annotators should mark atomic features first, then evaluate composite nodes based on those markings.
- Constraint reminders:
  - All node IDs are shown exactly as [BRACKETED] identifiers and must not be altered.

Atomic (single-condition) Nodes
- [USES-DISCOURSE-MARKERS]
  - Definition: A story is [USES-DISCOURSE-MARKERS] if the story uses discourse markers.
  - Quick check: Look for words or phrases that signal structure or transitions (e.g., however, meanwhile, on the other hand).

- [HAS-JUXTAPOSITION]
  - Definition: A story is [HAS-JUXTAPOSITION] if the story has juxtaposition.
  - Quick check: Identify clear placing of contrasting or side-by-side scenes, ideas, or images.

- [TOPIC-MYSTICAL-CREATURES]
  - Definition: A story is [TOPIC-MYSTICAL-CREATURES] if the story is about mystical creatures.
  - Quick check: The theme or central subject concerns creatures with supernatural or mythical qualities.

- [HAS-A-RED-HERRING]
  - Definition: A story is [HAS-A-RED-HERRING] if the story has a Red Herring.
  - Quick check: Spot misleading clues or elements intended to distract from the true explanation.

- [TOPIC-GIANT-CREATURES]
  - Definition: A story is [TOPIC-GIANT-CREATURES] if the story is about giant creatures.
  - Quick check: The narrative centers on very large creatures as a primary topic.

Composite (multi-condition) Nodes
- [FANTASTICAL-JUXTAPOSITION]
  - Definition: A story is [FANTASTICAL-JUXTAPOSITION] if both of the following are true:
    - The story is [TOPIC-MYSTICAL-CREATURES]
    - The story is [HAS-JUXTAPOSITION]
  - Logical form: [FANTASTICAL-JUXTAPOSITION] = AND([TOPIC-MYSTICAL-CREATURES], [HAS-JUXTAPOSITION])
  - Annotator action: Only mark this node when both constituent nodes have been independently marked true.

- [GIANT-MISDIRECTION]
  - Definition: A story is [GIANT-MISDIRECTION] if both of the following are true:
    - The story is [TOPIC-GIANT-CREATURES]
    - The story is [HAS-A-RED-HERRING]
  - Logical form: [GIANT-MISDIRECTION] = AND([TOPIC-GIANT-CREATURES], [HAS-A-RED-HERRING])
  - Annotator action: Only mark this node when both constituent nodes have been independently marked true.

Annotation Workflow (ordered, practical)
1. Read the story fully.
2. Mark atomic nodes first:
   - Evaluate and set truth for each of: [USES-DISCOURSE-MARKERS], [HAS-JUXTAPOSITION], [TOPIC-MYSTICAL-CREATURES], [HAS-A-RED-HERRING], [TOPIC-GIANT-CREATURES].
3. Evaluate composite nodes:
   - For [FANTASTICAL-JUXTAPOSITION], confirm both [TOPIC-MYSTICAL-CREATURES] and [HAS-JUXTAPOSITION] are true.
   - For [GIANT-MISDIRECTION], confirm both [TOPIC-GIANT-CREATURES] and [HAS-A-RED-HERRING] are true.
4. Record results; do not alter node IDs or logical relationships.

Notes and Tips for Consistency
- Composite nodes only become true when all listed constituent nodes are true (logical AND).
- Do not infer composite truth without first verifying each component independently.
- Keep labels exact: use the bracketed node IDs as given when recording or reporting annotations.
