[HAS-A-MORAL-LESSON]  
A story is [HAS-A-MORAL-LESSON] if the narrative explicitly or implicitly conveys a moral lesson (i.e., a normative takeaway intended to guide interpretation or behavior).

[USES-PERFECT-ASPECT]  
A story is [USES-PERFECT-ASPECT] if the text employs the perfect aspect (e.g., constructions analogous to “has/have/had + past participle”) as a grammatical mechanism in its narration.

[HAS-A-CLIFFHANGER]  
A story is [HAS-A-CLIFFHANGER] if it contains a cliffhanger—an intentionally unresolved situation or ending engineered to create suspense or deferred closure.

[THEME-KINDNESS]  
A story is [THEME-KINDNESS] if “Kindness” is a thematic throughline (i.e., kindness is a central idea motivating events, character decisions, or the narrative’s evaluative stance).

[STYLE-MODERN]  
A story is [STYLE-MODERN] if its stylistic register is modern (e.g., contemporary voice, conventions, or aesthetic).

[HAS-A-FLASHBACK]  
A story is [HAS-A-FLASHBACK] if it includes a flashback—an analeptic shift that narratively relocates the reader to earlier events relative to the main timeline.

[STYLE-CLASSIC]  
A story is [STYLE-CLASSIC] if its stylistic register is classic (e.g., traditional voice, conventions, or aesthetic).

[LONG-STORY]  
A story is [LONG-STORY] if its total word count is strictly greater than 375 words.

[HAS-JUXTAPOSITION]  
A story is [HAS-JUXTAPOSITION] if it uses juxtaposition, i.e., it places contrasting elements in proximity to highlight differences, tensions, or comparisons.

[STYLE-LIGHTHEARTED]  
A story is [STYLE-LIGHTHEARTED] if the dominant tone is lighthearted (i.e., primarily playful, humorous, or low-stakes in affect).
