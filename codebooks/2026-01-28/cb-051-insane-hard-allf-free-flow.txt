Think of this as a friendly, human-readable guide to the labels you’ll use when annotating stories. Each label below names a property and then says plainly when that property holds. I’ve kept every node ID exactly as it was and left the logical operators and relationships untouched — just smoothed the language so it flows better.

A story is [THEME-INNOVATION] if the story has the theme Innovation.

A story is [STYLE-CLASSIC] if the story is classic.

A story is [TOPIC-HIDDEN-TREASURES] if the story is about hidden treasures.

A story is [SHORT-STORY] if the story has fewer than 160 words.

A story is [STARTS-WITH-ADJECTIVE] if the story starts with an adjective.

A story is [THEME-HUMOR] if the story has the theme Humor.

A story is [LOW-READABILITY] if the story has readability score below 86.4.

A story is [STYLE-MODERN] if the story is modern.

A story is [TOPIC-MYSTICAL-CREATURES] if the story is about mystical creatures.

A story is [THEME-GROWTH] if the story has the theme Growth.

A story is [LONG-STORY] if the story has more than 375 words.

A story is [HAS-A-MORAL-LESSON] if the story has a moral lesson.

A story is [HAS-CIRCULAR-NARRATIVE-STRUCT] if the story has circular narrative structure.

A story is [THEME-HARDSHIP] if the story has the theme Hardship.

A story is [USES-CONDITIONAL-MOOD] if the story uses conditional mood.

A story is [USES-SUPERLATIVE-FORMS] if the story uses superlative forms.

A story is [THEME-HELPING-OTHERS] if the story has the theme Helping Others.

A story is [STYLE-PLAYFUL] if the story is playful.

A story is [HAS-JUXTAPOSITION] if the story has juxtaposition.

A story is [THEME-KINDNESS] if the story has the theme Kindness.

A story is [THEME-MAGIC] if the story has the theme Magic.

A story is [HAS-A-STORY-WITHIN-A-STORY] if the story has a story within a story.

A story is [TOPIC-TIME-TRAVEL] if the story is about time travel.

A story is [USES-WH-QUESTIONS] if the story uses wh-questions.

A story is [STYLE-HUMOROUS] if the story is humorous.

A story is [TOPIC-LOST-CIVILIZATIONS] if the story is about lost civilizations.

A story is [TOPIC-MAGICAL-LANDS] if the story is about magical lands.

A story is [STYLE-PHILOSOPHICAL] if the story is philosophical.

A story is [STYLE-CATEGORY] if the story's style is In the set { [STYLE-CLASSIC], [STYLE-MODERN], [STYLE-PLAYFUL], [STYLE-HUMOROUS], [STYLE-PHILOSOPHICAL] }.

A story is [TOPIC-MYSTICAL-SET] if the story's topic is In the set { [TOPIC-MYSTICAL-CREATURES], [TOPIC-MAGICAL-LANDS], [TOPIC-HIDDEN-TREASURES], [TOPIC-LOST-CIVILIZATIONS], [TOPIC-TIME-TRAVEL] }.

A story is [THEME-CLUSTER] if either of the following are true (Or):
- The story is [THEME-INNOVATION]
- The story is [THEME-GROWTH]
- The story is [THEME-HARDSHIP]
- The story is [THEME-HELPING-OTHERS]
- The story is [THEME-KINDNESS]
- The story is [THEME-MAGIC]
- The story is [THEME-HUMOR]

A story is [NARRATIVE-LENGTH-XOR] if exactly one (Xor) of the following is true:
- The story is [SHORT-STORY]
- The story is [LONG-STORY]

A story is [READABILITY-AND-START] if both of the following are true (And):
- The story is [LOW-READABILITY]
- The story is [STARTS-WITH-ADJECTIVE]

A story is [STRUCTURAL-DEVICE] if either of the following are true (Or):
- The story is [HAS-A-STORY-WITHIN-A-STORY]
- The story is [HAS-CIRCULAR-NARRATIVE-STRUCT]
- The story is [HAS-JUXTAPOSITION]

A story is [MORAL-OR-GROWTH] if either of the following are true (Or):
- The story is [HAS-A-MORAL-LESSON]
- The story is [THEME-GROWTH]

A story is [EQUAL-THEME-PRESENCE] if Equal(the truth of [THEME-GROWTH], the truth of [THEME-HARDSHIP]).

A story is [BALANCED-THEME] if both of the following are true (And):
- The story is [EQUAL-THEME-PRESENCE]
- Either of the following are true (Or):
  - The story is [THEME-KINDNESS]
  - The story is [THEME-HELPING-OTHERS]

A story is [THEME-SYNDROME] if both of the following are true (And):
- The story is [THEME-CLUSTER]
- Either of the following are true (Or):
  - The story is [BALANCED-THEME]
  - The story is [THEME-INNOVATION]

A story is [COMPLEX-STORY-TYPE] if all of the following are true:
- The story is [THEME-SYNDROME]
- The story is [STYLE-CATEGORY]
- The story is [TOPIC-MYSTICAL-SET]
- The story is [STRUCTURAL-DEVICE]
- Not the story is [STYLE-CLASSIC]
- The story is [NARRATIVE-LENGTH-XOR]
- The story is [READABILITY-AND-START]
- The story is [MORAL-OR-GROWTH]

That’s the set — each label is short and exact, but explained in a relaxed, readable voice so it’s easier to use when you’re deciding which tags apply to a story.
