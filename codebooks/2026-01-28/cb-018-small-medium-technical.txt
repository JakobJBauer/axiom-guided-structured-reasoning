[THEME-HELPING-OTHERS]
Definition: A story satisfies the predicate [THEME-HELPING-OTHERS] when its principal thematic content corresponds to "Helping Others."

[STYLE-PHILOSOPHICAL]
Definition: A story satisfies the predicate [STYLE-PHILOSOPHICAL] when its narrative style is philosophical in nature.

[STYLE-CLASSIC]
Definition: A story satisfies the predicate [STYLE-CLASSIC] when its stylistic characteristics conform to a classic style.

[HAS-JUXTAPOSITION]
Definition: A story satisfies the predicate [HAS-JUXTAPOSITION] when it contains juxtaposition as a structural or rhetorical device.

[STYLE-MODERN]
Definition: A story satisfies the predicate [STYLE-MODERN] when its stylistic characteristics conform to a modern style.

[MIXED-STYLE-JUXTAPOSE]
Definition: A story satisfies the predicate [MIXED-STYLE-JUXTAPOSE] if and only if both of the following conditions hold:
- The story satisfies [HAS-JUXTAPOSITION].
- Exactly one of the following is true:
  - The story satisfies [STYLE-CLASSIC].
  - The story satisfies [STYLE-MODERN].

[PHILOSOPHICAL-HELPING-OTHERS-DRAMATIC]
Definition: A story satisfies the predicate [PHILOSOPHICAL-HELPING-OTHERS-DRAMATIC] when at least one of the following disjoint cases applies:

Case A — Philosophical Helping (non-mixed):
- All of the following are true:
  - The story satisfies [STYLE-PHILOSOPHICAL].
  - The story satisfies [THEME-HELPING-OTHERS].
  - The story does not satisfy [MIXED-STYLE-JUXTAPOSE].

Case B — Mixed-style Juxtaposition without Helping Theme:
- Both of the following are true:
  - The story satisfies [MIXED-STYLE-JUXTAPOSE].
  - The story does not satisfy [THEME-HELPING-OTHERS].

Interpretation note: The predicate [PHILOSOPHICAL-HELPING-OTHERS-DRAMATIC] is true if either Case A or Case B holds; these cases are mutually exclusive due to the specified negations.
