[STYLE-LIGHTHEARTED]
Think of [STYLE-LIGHTHEARTED] as a simple label for stories that carry a light, breezy tone — the kind that makes you smile rather than sigh. A story is [STYLE-LIGHTHEARTED] if the story is lighthearted.

[STARTS-WITH-ADVERB]
When a tale opens with an adverb — "Suddenly," "Happily," "Reluctantly," and the like — we tag it as [STARTS-WITH-ADVERB]. A story is [STARTS-WITH-ADVERB] if the story starts with a adverb.

[LONG-STORY]
If a piece stretches beyond a modest length — more than 375 words — we call it [LONG-STORY]. A story is [LONG-STORY] if the story has more than 375 words.

[THEME-HUMOR]
Stories that center on humor, where comedy is the driving theme, get the [THEME-HUMOR] label. A story is [THEME-HUMOR] if the story has the theme Humor.

[TOPIC-BYGONE-ERAS]
When the subject matter drifts back in time to old-fashioned days and bygone eras, we use [TOPIC-BYGONE-ERAS]. A story is [TOPIC-BYGONE-ERAS] if the story is about bygone eras.

[WHIMSY-RETRO-COMEDY]
We reserve [WHIMSY-RETRO-COMEDY] for stories that combine a sunny tone with comedic intent, especially when they nod to the past or open with a playful adverb. Formally, a story is [WHIMSY-RETRO-COMEDY] if both of the following are true:
- The story is [STYLE-LIGHTHEARTED]
- The story is [THEME-HUMOR] and either of the following is true:
  - The story is [TOPIC-BYGONE-ERAS]
  - The story is [STARTS-WITH-ADVERB]
