[STYLE-MINIMALIST]
A story is [STYLE-MINIMALIST] if it conforms to minimalist stylistic criteria (i.e., economy of language, sparse detail, and restrained narrative elements).

[HIGH-READABILITY]
A story is [HIGH-READABILITY] if the story's readability metric exceeds 96.1.

[USES-SUPERLATIVE-FORMS]
A story is [USES-SUPERLATIVE-FORMS] if the narrative employs superlative grammatical forms (e.g., "best," "most," "least") as a salient stylistic device.

[THEME-GROWTH]
A story is [THEME-GROWTH] if the dominant thematic element of the narrative is Growth.

[TOPIC-TIME-TRAVEL]
A story is [TOPIC-TIME-TRAVEL] if the narrative content concerns time travel.

[TOPIC-HIDDEN-TREASURES]
A story is [TOPIC-HIDDEN-TREASURES] if the narrative content concerns hidden treasures.

[CLEAR-CONCISE]
A story is [CLEAR-CONCISE] if both of the following are true:
- The story is [STYLE-MINIMALIST]
- The story is [HIGH-READABILITY]

[EMPHATIC]
A story is [EMPHATIC] if both of the following are true:
- The story is [USES-SUPERLATIVE-FORMS]
- The story is not [STYLE-MINIMALIST]

[THEMATIC-ARC]
A story is [THEMATIC-ARC] if both of the following are true:
- The story is [THEME-GROWTH]
- Either of the following is true:
  - The story is [TOPIC-TIME-TRAVEL]
  - The story is [TOPIC-HIDDEN-TREASURES]

[COMPELLING]
A story is [COMPELLING] if both of the following are true:
- The story is [CLEAR-CONCISE]
- Either of the following is true:
  - The story is [EMPHATIC]
  - The story is [THEMATIC-ARC]
