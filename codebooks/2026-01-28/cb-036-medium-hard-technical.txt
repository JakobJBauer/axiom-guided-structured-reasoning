[LOW-READABILITY]
Definition: A story is classified as [LOW-READABILITY] if, and only if, its computed readability score is below 86.4.
Operational note: The readability metric referenced is the numeric score produced by the chosen readability algorithm; the threshold is strictly < 86.4.

[TOPIC-TIME-TRAVEL]
Definition: A story is classified as [TOPIC-TIME-TRAVEL] if its primary subject matter concerns time travel.

[TOPIC-SEASONAL-CHANGES]
Definition: A story is classified as [TOPIC-SEASONAL-CHANGES] if its primary subject matter concerns seasonal changes.

[USES-DISCOURSE-MARKERS]
Definition: A story is classified as [USES-DISCOURSE-MARKERS] if it contains discourse markers (e.g., transitional phrases, connective adverbs) as identifiable linguistic elements.

[STARTS-WITH-ADJECTIVE]
Definition: A story is classified as [STARTS-WITH-ADJECTIVE] if its first lexical token is an adjective.

[TOPIC-HIDDEN-TREASURES]
Definition: A story is classified as [TOPIC-HIDDEN-TREASURES] if its primary subject matter concerns hidden treasures.

[THEME-DECEPTION]
Definition: A story is classified as [THEME-DECEPTION] if deception is a central thematic element within the narrative.

[THEME-HELPING-OTHERS]
Definition: A story is classified as [THEME-HELPING-OTHERS] if helping others is a central thematic element within the narrative.

[STARTS-WITH-PREPOSITION]
Definition: A story is classified as [STARTS-WITH-PREPOSITION] if its first lexical token is a preposition.

[STARTS-WITH-ADVERB]
Definition: A story is classified as [STARTS-WITH-ADVERB] if its first lexical token is an adverb.

[NARRATIVE-SURFACE]
Definition: A story is classified as [NARRATIVE-SURFACE] if and only if one of the following structural conditions holds:
- Exactly one of the following is true:
  - The story is [STARTS-WITH-ADJECTIVE]
  - The story is [STARTS-WITH-ADVERB]
- Both of the following are true:
  - The story is [USES-DISCOURSE-MARKERS]
  - Either of the following is true:
    - The story is [LOW-READABILITY]
    - The story is [STARTS-WITH-PREPOSITION]

Interpretation: This node encodes an exclusive-or condition on sentence-initial part-of-speech OR a conjunction that requires presence of discourse markers plus either low readability or a prepositional start.

[INSCRUTABLE-THEME-STRUCTURE]
Definition: A story is classified as [INSCRUTABLE-THEME-STRUCTURE] if and only if both of the following top-level conditions are satisfied:
- Either of the following is true:
  - Both of the following are true:
    - The story is [TOPIC-TIME-TRAVEL]
    - Exactly one of the following is true:
      - The story is [THEME-DECEPTION]
      - The story is [THEME-HELPING-OTHERS]
  - Both of the following are true:
    - The story is [TOPIC-SEASONAL-CHANGES]
    - Both of the following are true:
      - The story is [TOPIC-HIDDEN-TREASURES]
      - The story is not [TOPIC-TIME-TRAVEL]
- The story is [NARRATIVE-SURFACE]

Interpretation: This composite node requires the narrative surface conditions to hold and, in addition, requires either a time-travel topic with an exclusive thematic choice between deception and helping-others, or a seasonal-changes topic that co-occurs with hidden-treasures while explicitly excluding time-travel.
