## Codebook (Structured)

Use the sections below to determine which node(s) apply to a story. Each node preserves the exact logical meaning and dependencies from the original codebook.

---

### 1) Core Theme Nodes

#### [THEME-HELPING-OTHERS]
A story is **[THEME-HELPING-OTHERS]** if:
- The story has the theme **Helping Others**.

---

### 2) Style Nodes

#### [STYLE-PHILOSOPHICAL]
A story is **[STYLE-PHILOSOPHICAL]** if:
- The story is philosophical.

#### [STYLE-CLASSIC]
A story is **[STYLE-CLASSIC]** if:
- The story is classic.

#### [STYLE-MODERN]
A story is **[STYLE-MODERN]** if:
- The story is modern.

---

### 3) Literary Device Node

#### [HAS-JUXTAPOSITION]
A story is **[HAS-JUXTAPOSITION]** if:
- The story has juxtaposition.

---

### 4) Composite / Derived Node (Mixed Style + Juxtaposition)

#### [MIXED-STYLE-JUXTAPOSE]
A story is **[MIXED-STYLE-JUXTAPOSE]** if **both** of the following are true:
- The story is **[HAS-JUXTAPOSITION]**
- **Exactly one** of the following is true:
  - The story is **[STYLE-CLASSIC]**
  - The story is **[STYLE-MODERN]**

*(Interpretation hint for “exactly one” only: classic XOR modern—one must be true and the other must be false.)*

---

### 5) High-Level Label Node (Main Decision Logic)

#### [PHILOSOPHICAL-HELPING-OTHERS-DRAMATIC]
A story is **[PHILOSOPHICAL-HELPING-OTHERS-DRAMATIC]** if **either** of the following is true:

- **Route A: Philosophical + Helping Others, but NOT the mixed juxtaposition condition**
  - **All** of the following are true:
    - The story is **[STYLE-PHILOSOPHICAL]**
    - The story is **[THEME-HELPING-OTHERS]**
    - The story is **not [MIXED-STYLE-JUXTAPOSE]**

**OR**

- **Route B: Mixed-style juxtaposition, but NOT Helping Others**
  - **Both** of the following are true:
    - The story is **[MIXED-STYLE-JUXTAPOSE]**
    - The story is **not [THEME-HELPING-OTHERS]**
