[MANY-PARAGRAPHS]  
A story is [MANY-PARAGRAPHS] iff it contains strictly more than 6 paragraphs (i.e., paragraph count > 6).

[HAS-A-STORY-WITHIN-A-STORY]  
A story is [HAS-A-STORY-WITHIN-A-STORY] iff it includes an embedded narrative (i.e., a story-within-the-story).

[STARTS-WITH-NOUN]  
A story is [STARTS-WITH-NOUN] iff its initial token is a noun (opening word is tagged as NOUN).

[USES-SUPERLATIVE-FORMS]  
A story is [USES-SUPERLATIVE-FORMS] iff it contains one or more superlative morphological forms (e.g., “-est”, “most + adjective”).

[HAS-CHECKHOVS-GUN]  
A story is [HAS-CHECKHOVS-GUN] iff it exhibits Chekhov’s gun: an element introduced earlier that is later used with clear narrative payoff.

[THEME-MAGIC]  
A story is [THEME-MAGIC] iff “Magic” is a central thematic axis of the narrative.

[USES-CONDITIONAL-MOOD]  
A story is [USES-CONDITIONAL-MOOD] iff it uses conditional mood constructions (e.g., “would”, “could”, “if … then …”).

[USES-WH-QUESTIONS]  
A story is [USES-WH-QUESTIONS] iff it contains wh-interrogatives (e.g., who/what/when/where/why/how questions).

[TOPIC-THE-ARTS]

[LONG-STORY]

[LOW-READABILITY]
