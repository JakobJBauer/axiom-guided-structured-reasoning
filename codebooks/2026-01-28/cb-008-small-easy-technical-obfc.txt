[attr-4]
Definition: Predicate indicating that the primary topical focus of the story is cultural traditions. A story satisfies [attr-4] if and only if the narrative content explicitly addresses, depicts, or centers on cultural traditions (rituals, customs, practices, or heritage-related activities).

[attr-1]
Definition: Predicate indicating the presence of an explicit moral or didactic conclusion. A story satisfies [attr-1] if and only if the narrative contains a stated or strongly implied moral lesson intended to instruct, guide behavior, or convey ethical principles.

[attr-2]
Definition: Predicate indicating that the story text begins with an adverb. A story satisfies [attr-2] if and only if the initial lexical unit of the text is an adverb (e.g., as determined by standard part-of-speech tagging or equivalent syntactic analysis).

[attr-3]
Definition: Predicate indicating the thematic classification "Magic." A story satisfies [attr-3] if and only if magic (supernatural manipulation, sorcery, enchanted phenomena, or related motifs) constitutes a central theme of the narrative.

[attr-5]
Definition: Composite predicate. A story satisfies [attr-5] if all of the following are true:
- The story is [attr-4]
- The story is [attr-3]
- The story is [attr-1]
