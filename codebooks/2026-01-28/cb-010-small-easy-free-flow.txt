[THEME-INNOVATION]  
Use [THEME-INNOVATION] when the story’s underlying theme is innovation—when it’s clearly centered on new ideas, new approaches, or change driven by inventive thinking.

[TOPIC-CULTURAL-TRADITIONS]  
Tag a story as [TOPIC-CULTURAL-TRADITIONS] whenever it’s focused on cultural traditions—customs, heritage practices, rituals, or shared ways of life passed through a culture.

[TOPIC-BYGONE-ERAS]  
Apply [TOPIC-BYGONE-ERAS] if the story is about bygone eras, meaning it’s oriented toward earlier times, past periods, or historical “back then” settings and perspectives.

[USES-WH-QUESTIONS]  
Mark [USES-WH-QUESTIONS] when the story uses wh-questions—questions that begin with words like who, what, when, where, why, or how.

[STARTS-WITH-NOUN]  
Choose [STARTS-WITH-NOUN] if the story begins with a noun—its opening word is a person, place, thing, or other noun form.

[STARTS-WITH-PREPOSITION]  
Choose [STARTS-WITH-PREPOSITION] if the story kicks off with a preposition—an opener like “in,” “on,” “at,” “under,” “between,” and similar.

- Either of the following is true:  
  - The story is [TOPIC-CULTURAL-TRADITIONS]  
  - The story is [TOPIC-BYGONE-ERAS]  
- The story is [USES-WH-QUESTIONS]
